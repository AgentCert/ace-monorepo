**Dataset**: data/input/21-05-ravi-top10 | **Runs**: 10 | **Fault types**: 3

| Fault Type | Target Service | Runs |
|---|---|---|
| pod-cpu-hog | carts pod | 10 |
| pod-memory-hog | carts pod | 10 |

| Fault Type | Detected | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| pod-cpu-hog | 9/10 | 90% |
| pod-memory-hog | 8/10 | 80% |

### Time-to-Detect (where detected)

| Fault Type | Mean TTD | Median TTD | Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| pod-cpu-hog | 5.8s | 4.5s | 1s – 13s |
| pod-memory-hog | 35s | 42s | 0.4s – 59s |
| pod-network-loss | — | — | — (not detected) |

### Time-to-Mitigate

No mitigation was recorded for any fault across all 30 runs. The agent operated in observation/diagnosis mode only.

### Observations

- **CPU hog is detected quickly and reliably**: 90% detection rate, most runs under 7s. The agent identifies CPU pressure via `pods_top` and PromQL queries.
- **Memory hog detection is slow and variable**: 80% rate but TTD spans 0.4s–59s, indicating inconsistent detection paths.
- **Network loss is a complete blind spot**: 0/10 runs detected. Later resolved by removing the `input` field from spans to reduce token overhead.
- **No mitigation coverage**: TTM is null across the board — remediation actions were not wired up or not captured.

## Part B — Pipeline Validation Scenarios (notebook: metric_scorecard_pipeline_executed.ipynb)

These synthetic scenarios were used to validate the TTD/TTM scoring pipeline before applying it to real data. Each scenario tests a different edge case in the scoring logic.

**Fault types across all scenarios**: pod-cpu-hog, pod-memory-hog, pod-network-loss  
**SLA thresholds used**: pod-cpu-hog = 120s, pod-memory-hog = 90s, pod-network-loss = 180s

| # | Scenario | Runs | What it tests |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Normal — all detects, within SLA | 30 | Baseline: all VALID, all compliant. Cumulative score ~0.59. |
| 2 | Null-heavy (~70% MISSING) | 30 | High miss rate. Score drops to ~0.15 — detection-weighting penalizes misses correctly. |
| 3 | Low variance (TTD ~40% of SLA) | 30 | Tight cluster, no outliers. Score ~0.64, no quality flags. |
| 4 | SLA breach (some TTD > 1.5× SLA) | 30 | Mixed: some within SLA, some severe breaches. Score collapses to ~0.04. |
| 5 | Small sample (n=5) | 5 | Low confidence tier. `mixed_category_health` flag triggered due to category score spread. |
| 6 | Category imbalance | 30 | Resource category healthy; network category null-heavy. `mixed_category_health` flag. |
| 7a | Small N baseline (n=3, all good) | 3 | All within SLA. Score ~0.66, no flags. |
| 7b | Small N with SLA breach (n=3, one breach) | 3 | Last run breaches at 1.5× SLA. Score drops to 0.47, `skewed_distribution` flag triggered. |

### Real TTM Data (12-05-26-sequential-aarya-30run)

A separate real dataset (`ttm_by_run_category_fault.json`) with 31 runs was tested against the TTM metric. Result: **0% mitigation rate** — all 93 observations are MISSING. Same fault types (pod-cpu-hog, pod-memory-hog, pod-network-loss) and same SLA thresholds. Confirms that mitigation tracking was not functional in the sequential-aarya experiment.

### Observations

- **Detection-weighted scoring works as intended**: Null-heavy scenario correctly produces a near-zero cumulative score despite some valid detections — the multiplier encodes both "how often" and "how well".
- **SLA breach scoring is appropriately punishing**: A few severe breaches (>2× SLA) drive the score to near zero even when some runs pass, which is the desired safety-conservative behaviour.
- **`mixed_category_health` flag is reliable**: Triggers when resource and network categories diverge significantly (e.g., scenario 6: resource_score=0.63 vs network_score=0.16).
- **Small samples (n=3–5) still produce valid scores** but with conservative central tendency (mean only) and quality flags to signal low confidence.
